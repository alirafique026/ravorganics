Why Choose Organic

Organic farming has the potential to address not only hunger and malnutrition, but also poverty, water scarcity, climate change, and unsustainable production and consumption. Globally, nature is deteriorating at unparalleled speeds in human history. Approximately one million species are on the verge of extinction, with many facing extinction within the next few decades.

The severe environmental and health effects of input-intensive or industrial agriculture, as well as its inability to offer a reasonable wage for farmers, high reliance on fossil fuels, and high sensitivity to climate change, all point to the need for reform. The good news is that agriculture has the potential to provide us with solutions. We need to develop policies for food and agriculture that take into account all of humanity's problems. Such regulations and policy tools that promote sustainable farming are critical for supporting organic farmers who contribute to the common good by producing high-quality food for everyone.

Increasing Popularity of Organic Food

People have become more aware of the benefits of organic foods, which has increased their popularity. They are grown naturally with manure or compost and only natural pesticides and insecticides, with no chemical pesticides or fertilisers. Because children are more vulnerable to diseases caused by pesticide residues in food than adults, parents prefer to feed them organic foods. Another benefit of organic foods is that they are free of growth hormones and antibiotic residues. Animal feeds frequently contain growth hormones and antibiotics, which are then transmitted directly into animal foods such as meat, chicken, fish, eggs, and dairy products, as well as from foods to consumers. These procedures are prohibited in organic farming, and animals are fed outside in natural settings. Antibiotics that are found in commonly consumed foods are rendered useless in humans.
